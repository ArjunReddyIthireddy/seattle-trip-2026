CRITICAL ITINERARY STRATEGY
To cover Glacier NP (Montana), North Cascades, Mount Rainier, Crater Lake, Portland, Cannon Beach, and Olympic NP in just 6 days, this trip leverages overnight driving. The 5 adults must divide driving into 2-hour shifts during these night transits. This allows the kids (aged 3 and 10) to sleep through the massive distances, giving you maximal sightseeing time in the daylight. All city visits (Seattle downtown, Space Needle, etc.) are skipped.

Day 2 · Saturday, June 21, 2026
Glacier National Park + Overnight to North Cascades 🏔️
Explore Going-to-the-Sun Road, Logan Pass, and alpine lakes, then drive back west overnight!
Overnight Drive continuation
⏰
MDT TIME ZONE CHANGE
Note that when crossing the Idaho-Montana border, you will enter Mountain Daylight Time (MDT), losing 1 hour. Set your clocks forward.
  • 8:30 AM

    Freshen up at the West Glacier Visitor Center bathrooms. Brush teeth, wash faces, stretch legs.

  • 4:30 PM

    🚨 DEPART MONTANA: Overnight Drive to North Cascades NP (500 mi · 8.5 hrs)
    Head back west along US-2 W. Cross back into Pacific Time Zone (gain 1 hour).
    Route: Kalispell → Libby → Sandpoint → Spokane → Grand Coulee → Diablo Lake Safety: Driver rotation shift every 2 hours Target: Reach Diablo Lake at sunrise

Double Park Day

DOUBLE NATIONAL PARK CHALLENGE

Road Safety & Family Care Guide

Crucial guidelines for overnight driving, vehicle cargo management, and toddler care.

🚙 Jeep Grand Cherokee L Packing

  • Seating Plan: Seating for 7 people (5 adults + 2 car seats). Two adults in front, two adults + baby seat in the middle row, one 10-year-old + one adult in the third row.
  • Cargo Limits: With the 3rd row up, the trunk space is limited to ~17 cubic feet. It is impossible to fit 7 suitcases.
  • Actionable Fix: Limit everyone to one small carry-on/backpack. Alternatively, rent a roof box cargo carrier from Yakima/Thule in Seattle, or mail bulk luggage ahead to Redmond.
  • Offline Navigation: Cell signal is zero in national parks. Download offline Google Maps for Washington, Oregon, and Montana before you leave.

🌙 Night Drive Rotation Plan

  • Driver Shifts: Rotate drivers strictly every 2 hours. Never let a driver go longer. The passenger seat must remain awake to keep the driver engaged.
  • Hydration/Caffeine: Keep a thermos of hot tea/coffee handy. Avoid heavy carbs right before driving.
  • Rest Areas: Stop at major rest areas. Washington, Idaho, and Montana have well-lit state rest areas on I-90 and US-2. Use them to wash face and do light stretches.
  • Emergency Gear: Ensure a blanket, flashlight, basic tools, and first aid kit are in the Grand Cherokee.

👶 Toddler (3 Year Old) Travel Tips

  • Night Drive Comfort: The toddler will sleep. Use comfortable car seat inserts, neck cushions, and soft blankets. Have a white noise machine or phone app running to drown out road noise.
  • Altitude Warnings: Logan Pass (6,646 ft), Paradise (5,400 ft), and Hurricane Ridge (5,242 ft) are at high elevations. Ascend slowly, encourage hydration, and pack warm fleece hoodies with beanies.
  • Motion Sickness: The mountain roads (Going-to-the-Sun, Hurricane Ridge) are wind-prone and twisty. Drive slowly and keep toddler motion sickness remedies ready.
  • Diaper Logs: Restrooms are spaced out. Do diaper checks at every gas/rest stop without fail.
